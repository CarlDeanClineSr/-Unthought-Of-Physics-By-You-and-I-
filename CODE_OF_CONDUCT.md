# Code of Conduct

## Our Classroom Standards

This repository operates as a learning environment where we are all students and teachers together. Our standards reflect our commitment to academic rigor, mutual respect, and collaborative learning.

## Core Values

### Academic Integrity
- Present only factually accurate, academically approved information
- Cite sources appropriately
- Admit errors and learn from them
- Distinguish clearly between established fact and areas of investigation

### Intellectual Honesty
- No fiction or speculative claims presented as fact
- No metaphors or embellishments that obscure truth
- No exaggeration or dramatization
- Precise, clear communication at all times

### Collaborative Learning
- Respect all participants as both learners and teachers
- Ask questions without fear of judgment
- Share knowledge generously
- Support others in their learning journey

## Expected Behavior

Participants in this learning space are expected to:

1. **Maintain Academic Rigor**
   - Follow current academic standards
   - Use approved mathematical and physical principles
   - Provide evidence for claims
   - Update understanding as academic consensus evolves

2. **Communicate Clearly**
   - Use precise, technical language appropriately
   - Define terms when necessary
   - Avoid metaphorical or embellished language
   - Present information without fiction or speculation

3. **Foster Mutual Respect**
   - Treat all participants as equals in learning
   - Welcome questions and discussion
   - Provide constructive feedback
   - Acknowledge the contributions of others

4. **Pursue Truth**
   - Prioritize accuracy over convenience
   - Challenge ideas respectfully with evidence
   - Remain open to correction
   - Value understanding over ego

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not permitted:

1. **Academic Misconduct**
   - Presenting fiction as fact
   - Making unsupported claims
   - Falsifying or misrepresenting sources
   - Ignoring established academic standards

2. **Communication Issues**
   - Using metaphors or embellishments instead of clear explanation
   - Presenting speculation without proper disclaimer
   - Obfuscating with unnecessary complexity
   - Dramatic exaggeration

3. **Disrespectful Conduct**
   - Dismissing others' questions or learning efforts
   - Personal attacks or insults
   - Harassment of any kind
   - Discrimination

## Enforcement

### Process
1. Issues will be reviewed by project maintainers
2. Context and intent will be considered
3. Corrective action will be proportional to the violation
4. Repeat violations may result in removal from the learning space

### Consequences
- First violation: Private warning and guidance
- Second violation: Public warning
- Continued violations: Temporary or permanent removal

## Reporting

If you observe behavior that violates these standards:
1. Document the specific behavior
2. Open an issue or contact maintainers directly
3. Include relevant context
4. Be prepared to discuss the concern respectfully

## Our Pledge

We pledge to maintain this space as a classroom for real science, where:
- Academic standards guide all contributions
- Fiction, metaphors, and embellishing have no place
- We learn together as students and teachers
- Truth and understanding are our highest goals
