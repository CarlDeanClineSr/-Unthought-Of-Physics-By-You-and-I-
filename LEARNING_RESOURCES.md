# Curated Learning Resources

This document provides a starting point for learning physics and mathematics with academic rigor.

## Foundational Physics Textbooks

### Classical Mechanics
- **"Classical Mechanics"** by Herbert Goldstein, Charles Poole, and John Safko
  - Comprehensive graduate-level treatment
  - Standard reference for advanced mechanics
  
- **"An Introduction to Mechanics"** by Daniel Kleppner and Robert Kolenkow
  - Undergraduate level
  - Strong problem sets

### Electromagnetism
- **"Introduction to Electrodynamics"** by David J. Griffiths
  - Standard undergraduate text
  - Clear explanations with mathematical rigor

### Quantum Mechanics
- **"Introduction to Quantum Mechanics"** by David J. Griffiths
  - Accessible introduction with solid mathematics

### Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics
- **"Thermal Physics"** by Charles Kittel and Herbert Kroemer
  - Concise and rigorous approach

### General Relativity
- **"Gravitation"** by Charles W. Misner, Kip S. Thorne, and John Archibald Wheeler
  - Comprehensive reference
  - Advanced treatment

## Online Academic Resources

### Open Courseware
- **MIT OpenCourseWare** (ocw.mit.edu)
  - Free course materials from MIT
  - Includes lecture notes, problem sets, exams

- **Stanford Online** (online.stanford.edu)
  - Various physics courses available

### Video Lectures
- **MIT Physics Lectures** (YouTube: MIT OpenCourseWare)
  - Walter Lewin's classical mechanics and E&M courses
  - 8.01, 8.02, 8.03 series

### Databases and References
- **arXiv.org** (arxiv.org)
  - Preprint server for physics papers
  - Free access to latest research

- **NIST Physical Reference Data** (physics.nist.gov)
  - Authoritative physical constants
  - Standard reference values

## Mathematical Foundations

### Mathematical Methods for Physics
- **"Mathematical Methods for Physicists"** by George B. Arfken, Hans J. Weber, and Frank E. Harris
  - Comprehensive reference for mathematical techniques

- **"Mathematics for Physics"** by Michael Stone and Paul Goldbart
  - Modern treatment of mathematical physics

### Linear Algebra
- **"Linear Algebra Done Right"** by Sheldon Axler
  - Clear, proof-based approach

## Using These Resources

### Approach
1. Start with foundational concepts
2. Work through examples and problems
3. Verify understanding with calculations
4. Cross-reference multiple sources
5. Ask questions when concepts are unclear

### Academic Standards
- Follow the mathematical derivations
- Work problems yourself before checking solutions
- Maintain precision in calculations
- Question assumptions and understand limitations

### Note on Currency
Physics is a living field. While fundamental principles are well-established:
- New experimental results refine our understanding
- Computational methods continue to evolve
- Applications expand to new domains
- Mathematical formalisms may be improved

Always verify that your understanding aligns with current academic consensus.
